About Basse Area Council

The Capital and Regional Headquarters of the Upper River Division

One of the eight Local Government Areas in the Gambia. Basse is 370 kilometres from the capital of Banjul, and about 340 km by road from Yundum Airport. Basse has a bustling village market, a ferry crossing to the north bank for passengers and goods, and an important transit point for merchandise going to eastern Senegal, Mali and Guinea.

An Insight: Know Your Council

Jurisdictions of the Council

Subject to the provisions of the Councils Local Government Act, a Council shall, within its area of jurisdiction - provide services as it deems fit, and promote on a sound basis community development and self help

Road Network Infrastructure

A Council shall establish a Local Roads Authority for its area of jurisdiction which shall be responsible for the upkeep and maintenance of all secondary roads.

Local Government Act (page 37) (80.(1))

Public and Environmental Health

Subject to national policy guidelines and regulations, every Council shall be responsible for the promotion and preservation of health within its area of jurisdiction. There shall be Local Public Health Committees for each Local Government Area through which the Council shall perform health functions

Local Government Act (page 36) (76.(1))

Natural Resources

A Council shall, in its area of jurisdiction, be responsible for the management, protection and conservation of forest resources; the control, development and sustainable management of fisheries resources; and the conservation, sustainable management and bio-diversity of the environment;

Local Government Act (page 33) (71.(1))

Services of The Council

Essential Services of Basse Area Council

Sanitation

The Council is committed to providing comprehensive sanitation services that ensure the health and well-being of the people of Basse. Our range of services encompasses waste collection, disposal, and recycling programs. At the heart of our sanitation services is the dedication to maintaining clean and aesthetically pleasing public spaces. The council takes pride in offering street cleaning, public restroom maintenance, and graffiti removal services.

Read More

Agriculture

The Council's agricultural initiatives include access to modern farming technology, training programs, and workshops aimed at enhancing productivity and improving crop yields. Through strategic partnerships and the distribution of resources, we strive to empower our local farming community, ensuring food security, economic growth, and environmental preservation.

Read More

Community Service

Community service lies at the heart of the local government council's mission to foster unity, social cohesion, and inclusivity. Through a diverse range of programs, we encourage residents to actively engage in giving back to their neighborhoods. The council's community service initiatives include volunteering opportunities at local schools, elderly care initiatives, and environmental restoration projects.

Read More

Health

The Council's health initiatives focus on community well-being. We offer comprehensive healthcare programs, providing access to modern medical facilities, training, and workshops. Through strategic partnerships and resource distribution, we yearn to empower the community of Basse with tools for a healthier lifestyle, aiming for a robust healthcare system that promotes overall quality of life.

Read More

Education

The Council's education drive yearns to empowers the community of Basse with modern resources, training, and workshops oppurtunities. Through strategic partnerships, we aim to foster a dynamic education system that sparks creativity and critical thinking, creating pathways for a brighter future fot the people of Basse

Read More

Lands

The Council's efforts in land management encompass regulations, transfers, and ownership. We work to establish effective and fair land regulations, streamline land transfer processes, and ensure secure and responsible land ownership. Through community engagement and strategic planning, we aim to create a transparent and efficient system that supports sustainable land practices and community development.
